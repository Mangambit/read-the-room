Youth Code x AI · Track 03

Read the Room

Paste a confusing message. See what they really mean.

Built by Dariush

01

The problem

Some messages cost you a whole evening of overthinking.

"ok."   "fine, do whatever."   a passive-aggressive email. For neurodivergent people and anyone with social anxiety, reading tone isn't automatic, and getting it wrong has real stakes.

02

The idea

See through a message to what they actually meant.

The calm surface is what they said. The reveal underneath is what they meant: the real meaning, the tone, whether they're upset with you, and what to say back.

03

Demo · the decode

It reads between the lines.

  • What they really mean, in plain words
  • The exact words that gave it away, highlighted
  • Upset with you? · what they want · urgency · confidence
04

Demo · reply + check

It writes the reply, and checks yours.

  • Three tones (warm / professional / firm), streamed
  • Pick your goal: apologize · set a boundary · de-escalate
  • Check my reply: how it lands plus a warmer rewrite
05

Built to be read · this is the point

Made for neurodivergent minds first.

  • Set in Atkinson Hyperlegible, drawn for legibility
  • One tap: dyslexia spacing · high contrast · reduced motion
  • Never color-alone. No streaks, no scores, no judgment
  • A gentle crisis safety net when a message is heavy
06

The promise

Your messages are yours. We never store them.

Read once to help you, then discarded. No account, no history, nothing kept on a server, and the server never logs your messages.

07

How it works · the engineering

One adapter, a chain of brains.

Cerebras · primary Groq · fallback Demo-safe · offline
  • A capped or slow provider rolls to the next, automatically
  • Strict JSON (Zod) plus a crash-proof tolerant parser
  • The live demo can't dead-end: an offline canned mode is the last resort
  • Works as a browser extension too, decode text on any page
08

Why it matters

A tool you can use tomorrow, built first for the people the internet forgets.

It turns a moment of anxiety into a moment of understanding. That's the whole job.
