# Demo video script — Read the Room

Target length **2:30**. Screen recording + voiceover (your voice, calm and real — not
an ad). Record with `LLM_PROVIDER=demo` so nothing can fail, or with Groq for live
reads. Use the 6 built-in example chips so every read is clean.

> Tone note: talk like you're showing a friend something you made, not pitching. Short
> sentences. No "revolutionary," no "seamless." Let the product do the talking.

---

### Scene 1 — The problem (0:00–0:18)
**On screen:** a phone with a real text: *"Per my last email, the assignment was due
Friday. Let me know if you need extra support going forward."* Hold on it.

**You (VO):**
> "We've all gotten a message like this and just... stared at it. Are they mad? Do I
> apologize? For a lot of people — especially if you're neurodivergent — reading between
> the lines isn't automatic. It can cost you a whole night."

### Scene 2 — The decode (0:18–0:50)
**On screen:** open Read the Room. Paste the message, tag "Teacher," click **Read the
room**. The deep-plum reveal slides in: *"They're annoyed you missed the deadline... the
'extra support' is a polite jab, not a real offer."* Then the read-outs: upset **Yes**,
88% sure, what they want, urgency **High**.

**You (VO):**
> "This is Read the Room. Paste the message, and it shows you what they actually mean —
> the tone, whether they're upset with *you*, what they want, and how urgent it is. In
> plain language. No guessing."

### Scene 3 — The reply (0:50–1:15)
**On screen:** scroll to **Your reply**. Show the Warm draft streaming in. Click between
**Warm / Professional / Firm**. Hit **Copy**.

**You (VO):**
> "And it drafts the reply for you — warm, professional, or firm, whatever fits. One tap
> to copy. You're not staring at a blank box anymore."

### Scene 4 — Check my reply (1:15–1:40)
**On screen:** switch to **Check my reply**. Type your own draft: *"k. whatever works."*
Click **Check how it lands**. Show the plum *"How this will land"* + the warmer rewrite.

**You (VO):**
> "It works the other way too. Before you send something, paste your own reply and it
> tells you how it'll land — and offers a kinder way to say the same thing."

### Scene 5 — Built for everyone (1:40–2:08)
**On screen:** open the **Accessibility** menu. Toggle **Dyslexia-friendly** and **High
contrast** — the page visibly changes. Then point at the footer: *"We never store your
messages."*

**You (VO):**
> "This is built to be read. One tap for a dyslexia-friendly font, high contrast,
> reduced motion. Nothing's stored — your messages are read once and gone. And if a
> message sounds genuinely heavy, it points you to real support instead of a glib reply.
> No judgment, ever."

### Scene 6 — Close (2:08–2:30)
**On screen:** the landing page hero — the message, the *"reading through ↓"* scan line,
the reveal. Hold.

**You (VO):**
> "Read the Room. For everyone who's ever overthought the reply. It's the friend who
> finally explains what they actually meant."

**End card:** `Read the Room` · *what they really mean* · https://read-the-room-phi.vercel.app
